About Credit Score

Your credit score is a numerical representation of your creditworthiness. In India, CIBIL scores range from 300 to 900, with higher scores indicating better credit health.

Factors Affecting Credit Score

  • Payment History (35%): Timely payments boost score
  • Credit Utilization (30%): Keep balance below 30% of limit
  • Credit Age (15%): Longer credit history is better
  • New Credit (10%): Too many inquiries hurt score
  • Credit Mix (10%): Different types of credit accounts

Credit Score Ranges

  • Excellent (800-900): Best rates and terms
  • Very Good (740-799): Good approval chances
  • Good (670-739): Average approval chances
  • Fair (580-669): Limited approval, higher rates
  • Poor (300-579): Difficult to get credit

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to improve credit score?

Credit score changes can be seen within days for positive actions like payments, but significant improvements take 3-6 months of consistent good financial behavior.

What hurts credit score the most?

Late payments, high credit utilization (>70%), too many credit inquiries, and account closures can significantly hurt your credit score.

How often should I check my credit score?

Check your credit score once every 3-6 months. Free annual reports are available from CIBIL. Avoid checking too frequently as some bureaus count soft inquiries.

Can I improve my credit score quickly?

Yes, paying down credit card balances, making all payments on time, and avoiding new credit applications can improve your score within 30-60 days.
