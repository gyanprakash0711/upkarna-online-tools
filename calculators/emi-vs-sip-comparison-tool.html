What is EMI vs SIP Comparison?

EMI vs SIP comparison helps you make informed financial decisions by comparing the cost of borrowing money (EMI) with the benefits of systematic investing (SIP). This tool shows whether taking a loan or investing the same amount gives better long-term financial outcomes.

How EMI vs SIP Calculator Works

Enter your loan details to calculate total EMI payments, and investment details to project SIP returns. The tool compares the total amount paid for EMI versus the future value of SIP investments, helping you understand which option is more beneficial for your financial goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the tax implications of EMI vs SIP?

EMI interest payments may be tax-deductible under Section 24(b) for home loans. SIP investments in ELSS funds offer tax deductions under Section 80C, and long-term capital gains are taxed at lower rates.

How does inflation affect EMI vs SIP comparison?

Inflation reduces the real value of EMI payments over time, making debt cheaper. However, SIP investments need to beat inflation to provide positive real returns.

Can I do both EMI and SIP simultaneously?

Yes, many people manage both. Use EMI for necessary purchases and SIP for wealth creation. Ensure your debt-to-income ratio remains healthy.

What are the risks involved in EMI vs SIP?

EMI risk is default leading to credit score damage. SIP risk includes market volatility and potential losses. Diversification and emergency funds help mitigate these risks.

How to calculate the break-even point between EMI and SIP?

The break-even point is when SIP returns equal the EMI interest cost. Use the formula: EMI Interest Rate = (SIP Future Value / Total SIP Invested)^(1/Time) - 1.
